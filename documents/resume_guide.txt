OVERVIEW

A strong resume is critical for getting shortlisted at top tech companies.
Many qualified candidates fail to get interviews due to poorly structured
resumes, not lack of qualifications.

Four steps to a strong software engineering resume:
1. Set up an ATS-friendly resume template
2. Fill with well-framed content in a meaningful order
3. Optimize with keywords from job descriptions
4. Test using free tools

RESUME STRUCTURE (in order)

1. Professional summary / headline
2. Contact information
3. Skills (programming languages, frameworks, databases)
4. Work Experience (reverse chronological)
5. Education (put first if still in school or under 3 years experience)
6. Projects
7. Awards, Certifications (optional)

PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY

Keep under 50 words. Answer why you are a good fit. Use active voice and
action words. Start with your job title noun (e.g. "Software Engineer").

Example headline:
"Senior Year student at University X with a focus on AI and ML. Interned
at X companies in full stack and ML engineering roles."

CONTACT INFORMATION

Must-haves: name, phone, location, email, LinkedIn
Good-to-haves: GitHub URL, personal website, competitive coding profile

SKILLS SECTION

Format: [Category] : [Skill | Skill | Skill]
Include programming languages, frameworks, databases.

WORK EXPERIENCE

Format:
[Company], [Location] | [Job Title] | [MM/YYYY - MM/YYYY]

List accomplishments as:
[Summary] : [Action] that resulted in [quantifiable outcome]

PROJECTS SECTION

Include at least 2 projects. Link each to GitHub or a live demo.
Outline your specific contributions and the technologies used.

Example:
"Maintainer and lead engineer for Docusaurus v2, a static site generator
powering documentation for React Native, Jest, and Relay. Used by 7,600+
projects on GitHub."

EDUCATION SECTION

Format:
[Degree], [Graduation Year]
[University], [Location]
GPA: X.XX / 4.0 (only list if above 3.5)
Key achievements, leadership, awards

KEYWORD OPTIMIZATION

- Analyze job descriptions for must-have and nice-to-have skills
- Add matching keywords to Skills, Work Experience, and Education sections
- Use full names of abbreviations: "Amazon Web Services" not just "AWS"
- Do not keyword stuff — a human will read the resume eventually
- Tailor your resume for each application type

RESUME TIPS

- Keep to one page
- Highlight a few strong achievements rather than many average ones
- Use standard fonts: Arial, Calibri, or Garamond, minimum 10pt
- Create in Google Docs or Microsoft Word, submit as PDF
- Do not use graphic design tools or online resume builders
- Reduce margins to 0.5 inches rather than using headers/footers

COVER LETTER

- Tailor to each company and role
- Do not repeat your resume — complement it
- Structure: hook, your value, brief relevant story
- Keep under one page
- Proofread carefully
