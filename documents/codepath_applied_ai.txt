OVERVIEW

CodePath's Applied AI Engineering pathway equips students to code, build, and
collaborate in AI-powered environments. The program embeds AI directly into
programming practice rather than treating it as a separate topic. Students
strengthen CS fundamentals while learning to use AI tools for debugging,
scaffolding, and refinement, always with human oversight.

The pathway is a 3-course progression:
- AI110: Foundations of AI Engineering
- AI201: Applications of AI Engineering
- AI301: AI Open Source Capstone

All courses are no-cost, 10 weeks, instructor-led, and virtual.
Time commitment: 4-9 hours per week depending on level.

WHAT YOU LEARN

Foundations (AI110):
- Data structures, algorithms, and OOP in Python
- Critical evaluation and refinement of AI-generated code
- Algorithmic thinking and prompt engineering
- Machine learning literacy: supervised, unsupervised, generative models
- Intro to RAG, agentic workflows, fine-tuning, and AI guardrails
- Git and GitHub collaboration workflows

Applications (AI201):
- AI-augmented work in large-scale production systems
- Intermediate programming with AI as a collaborator

Advanced/Capstone (AI301):
- Contribute to real open-source repositories using Claude Code
- Spec-driven development and debugging
- RAG, agents, fine-tuning, and guardrails at production level
- Portfolio-ready public contributions employers can see

WHAT AI-ASSISTED PROGRAMMING MEANS IN PRACTICE

- Use AI to generate or refactor code from specifications
- Debug with AI while verifying correctness and intent
- Critique and improve AI outputs
- Collaborate with AI throughout design, documentation, and testing
- The human is always in charge, but AI is always in the loop

HOW THIS HELPS IN INTERVIEWS

Employers increasingly ask candidates to interpret, review, and explain
AI-generated code. This course builds those skills alongside debugging,
testing, and explaining design decisions. Students also build portfolio
projects and open source contributions for technical interviews.

ELIGIBILITY

- 18 years or older, located in the US
- Enrolled in a CS or software-related degree program
- At least one intro programming course completed
- 4-9 hours per week available including class time

HOW TO APPLY

Two steps:
1. Fill out a 10-15 minute application. Requires a GitHub account.
2. Complete a 90-minute HackerRank assessment.

Decisions sent within two weeks of HackerRank submission.
