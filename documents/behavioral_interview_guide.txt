OVERVIEW

Behavioral interviews may seem unstructured but interviewers are trying to get
to know you better. There is always a set of common questions asked across top
tech companies. Use the STAR format to answer them: Situation, Task, Action, Result.

TOP 30 MOST COMMON BEHAVIORAL QUESTIONS

- Why do you want to work for X company?
- What are you looking for in your next role?
- Tell me about a time when you had a conflict with a co-worker.
- Tell me about a time you had to influence somebody else.
- What is the most challenging aspect of your current project?
- What was the most difficult bug you fixed in the past 6 months?
- How do you tackle challenges? Name a difficult challenge, how you overcame it,
  and what you learned.
- What frustrates you?
- Tell me about a time you had a disagreement with your manager.
- Talk about a project you are most passionate about or did your best work on.
- What does your best day of work look like?
- What is the most constructive feedback you have received in your career?
- What is something you had to persevere at for multiple months?
- Tell me about a time you met a tight deadline.
- What aspects of your work are most often criticized?
- How have you handled criticism of your work?
- What strengths do you think are most important for your job position?
- What words would your colleagues use to describe you?
- What would you hope to achieve in the first six months after being hired?
- Tell me why you will be a good fit for the position.

AMAZON BEHAVIORAL QUESTIONS

- How do you deal with a failed deadline?
- Tell me about a situation where you had a conflict with a teammate.
- Tell me a situation where you would have done something differently.
- What is the most difficult mistake you have made?
- How would you improve Amazon's website?

GENERAL TIPS FOR BEHAVIORAL INTERVIEWS

- Use the STAR format: Situation, Task, Action, Result
- Prepare 5-6 strong stories from past experiences that can answer multiple questions
- Stories should show impact, leadership, conflict resolution, and growth
- Tailor your answers to the company's values and mission
- Practice out loud before the interview
- Be specific — vague answers score poorly
- Show self-awareness when discussing weaknesses or failures
- Always end with what you learned or how you grew

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

- Giving generic answers not tied to real experiences
- Focusing on the team instead of your individual contribution
- Not quantifying impact when possible
- Rambling without a clear structure
- Badmouthing previous employers or managers
- Forgetting to mention the outcome of the situation
