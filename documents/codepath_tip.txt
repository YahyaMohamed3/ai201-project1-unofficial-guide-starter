OVERVIEW

CodePath's Technical Interview Prep (TIP) is a no-cost 10-week virtual course
led by professional engineers from companies like Google, Meta, and Amazon.
It prepares students to solve algorithmic problems in technical interviews at
top tech companies. The course meets twice a week and requires 5-13 hours per
week depending on level.

The course is offered at three levels:
- TIP101: Intro (5-10 hours/week)
- TIP102: Intermediate (6-10 hours/week)
- TIP103: Advanced (8-13 hours/week)

WHAT YOU LEARN

- The UMPIRE Problem Solving Process: Understand, Match, Plan, Implement,
  Review, Evaluate
- Classic algorithms: sorting, searching, traversal, hashmaps
- Big O notation and runtime complexity estimation
- Common coding interview patterns
- Data structures: Binary Trees, Heaps
- Dynamic programming, recursion, combinations
- Object Oriented Design and Systems Design basics
- AI as a debugging and research assistant during problem solving

COURSE LEVELS

Intro (TIP101): Covers Python programming basics and foundational CS concepts.
Adaptive pace. 10 units covering fundamentals and meta-learning for internships.

Intermediate (TIP102): Focuses on the UMPIRE technique, advanced data
structures, dynamic programming, and Big O. For students with basic programming
and algorithm knowledge.

Advanced (TIP103): Comprehensive 10-unit program covering Big O, recursion,
dynamic programming, graph algorithms, and FAANG-level interview strategies.
Requires foundational programming and data structure knowledge.

ELIGIBILITY

- 18 years or older
- Enrolled in a CS or software-related degree program in the US
- Completed at least one basic programming course
- Knowledge of algorithms and data structures (for Intermediate/Advanced)
- Available 5-13 hours per week including class time

HOW TO APPLY

Two steps:
1. Fill out a 10-15 minute application about programming experience and goals.
   Requires a GitHub account.
2. Complete a 120-minute HackerRank calibration assessment.

Most applicants hear back within two weeks of submitting the assessment.

WHY IT MATTERS

- Software engineer starting salaries average $100K+ in many markets
- 70% of tech employers expect candidates to be comfortable with AI workflows
- CodePath is a nonprofit backed by Amazon, Google, Meta, and Salesforce,
  which is why the course is offered at no cost
- Students receive a certificate of completion to share on LinkedIn
