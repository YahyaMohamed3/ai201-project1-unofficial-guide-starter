OVERVIEW

Coding Interview University is a multi-month study plan for becoming a software
engineer at a large company. The items listed prepare you for technical interviews
at companies including Amazon, Facebook, Google, and Microsoft.

You are not being hired for knowledge, but for how you apply the knowledge.

PROGRAMMING LANGUAGE CHOICE

Choose one language and stick with it for interviews. Solid choices are:
- Python: Modern, expressive, less code to write in interviews
- Java: Common in enterprise, well-supported in interviews
- C++: Performant, common in systems and competitive programming

You need to be very comfortable in your chosen language.

TOPICS TO STUDY

Core data structures:
- Arrays: time O(1) for index/update, O(n) for insert/remove elsewhere
- Linked Lists: singly and doubly linked
- Stack: LIFO, trivial to implement with an array
- Queue: FIFO, implement with linked list or fixed array
- Hash Table: hashing with chaining or open addressing

Algorithms:
- Binary Search: O(log n), requires sorted input
- Sorting: mergesort O(n log n), quicksort O(n log n) average,
  selection/insertion sort O(n^2). Know stability and tradeoffs.
- Graph traversal: BFS and DFS, know complexity and tradeoffs
- Dynamic Programming: recognize problems that can be broken into subproblems
- Recursion and backtracking

Additional topics:
- Big-O / Asymptotic analysis: express complexity in terms of input size
- Trees: binary search trees, heaps, traversals (preorder, inorder, postorder)
- Processes and Threads: difference, deadlock, concurrency basics

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. Do not just watch videos and take notes without practicing problems.
   Start coding interview questions while you are still learning each topic.
   Apply what you learn immediately or you will forget it.

2. Do not try to memorize answers. Practice recognizing problem patterns
   and how to apply the right data structure or algorithm.

3. Focus. Distractions waste valuable study time.

BEHAVIORAL / INTERVIEW PREP

Think of 20 interview questions you might face. Prepare a story, not just data.

Common questions:
- Why do you want this job?
- What is a tough problem you solved?
- What was the biggest challenge you faced at a project or job?
- What did you learn from a past project?
- How do you work best individually and as part of a team?

QUESTIONS TO ASK THE INTERVIEWER

- How large is your team?
- What does your development cycle look like?
- How are decisions made in your team?
- What are you currently working on?
- How is work/life balance on the team?
